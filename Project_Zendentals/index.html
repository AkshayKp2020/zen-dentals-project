The mouth is indespensible to Eating, Speaking, Smiling and Quality of Life

August 29 2020

The pandamic has understandably been a strange time to go to dentist office, and it can be even more disconcerting for many parents to take their little ones for their routine check ups. However, it need not be the case. According to Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) “There are currently no data available to assess the risk of SARS-CoV-2 transmission during dental practice.” Furthermore, it emphasizes that the oral health care should be a public health priority in the response to the pandemic.
